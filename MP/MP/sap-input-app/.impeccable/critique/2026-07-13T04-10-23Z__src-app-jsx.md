### Overall Impression
A highly professional, industrial, yet modern login page that successfully establishes trust. The 50/50 layout split works well, but the branding panel is overly dense and the page lacks a clear support path for struggling users.

### What's Working
1. **Strong Brand Authority:** The sequence of official logos (Danantara -> BUMN -> Holding -> PTPN) and the bold typography establish immediate credibility.
2. **Clear Layout Distinction:** The 50/50 split effectively separates the atmospheric brand messaging from the interactive login task.
3. **Atmospheric Context:** The floating equipment names and animated background images provide a very specific, grounded context (Plant Maintenance) rather than generic corporate stock photos.

### Priority Issues

- **[P2] No Help or Support Path**
  - **Why it matters:** If users forget their NIK/password or hit an error, they have no clear resolution path, leading to frustration and external support tickets.
  - **Fix:** Add a subtle "Butuh bantuan?" link directing to IT support or a helpdesk below the login form.
  - **Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

- **[P3] Gray text on colored background**
  - **Why it matters:** `text-slate-500` on `bg-red-50` washes out legibility and contrast, creating a "muddy" appearance.
  - **Fix:** Ensure text contrast is sharp, using darker colors like `text-red-700` for errors.
  - **Suggested command:** `$impeccable polish`

- **[P3] Left panel visual density**
  - **Why it matters:** While striking, the combination of floating tags, text paragraphs, bullet points, and background images is very dense information competing for attention on the non-interactive side.
  - **Fix:** Reduce the opacity or number of floating tags slightly to allow the core message to breathe more.
  - **Suggested command:** `$impeccable quieter`

### Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer)**: May be overwhelmed by the density of the left panel information and might not know who to contact if their NIK doesn't work (due to lack of help links).

**Alex (Power User)**: Might expect the NIK field to auto-focus on page load for immediate typing and efficiency.
